WHO AM I?
  • I’m not someone who likes to be in the lime light, or be in the hot seat. I wouldn’t go as far as to say that I’m an introvert, I just don’t like bringing attention to myself. I’m totally fine with just sitting on the sidelines, and letting others take the spotlight. I have always been very laid back, and patient person, but my mission was where I was truly gifted the gift of patience. I served in the Philippines, which meant that my companions would be Filipinos. People who barely speak English, and were raised in a very different culture. And that was a huge hurdle for me at first. Filipinos are pretty different than Americans. And it took some getting used to. But with God’s help, I was gifted patience. And a lot of it. And the patient helped me be an understanding, and great companion to those I was assigned to serve with. It also helped in my marriage. My wife would get annoyed sometimes because I was so patient with her, and with other people. She would say things like “How did that driver cutting you off not annoy you?” or “How are you so calm right now, I’m freaking out!” (After we learned I needed to have an operation done on my heart for my WPW syndrome.) I don’t take any credit for my extreme patience, as it was truly a gift from God.
    But there are of course are many things about myself that I need to improve on. One of those, is sometimes I have the tendency to rationalize my sins. There are times where I see someone else sin, and think “Wow I can't believe they did that". But then if I go and do that same sin, it’s all the sudden totally ok, and I find some way to rationalize it. I don’t mean like huge terrible sins, but little things here and there. And the little things are what get us in the end! I try and rationalize things by thinking, “Oh, well, I’ve had a hard day, so it as ok that I didn’t pray at all, or that I didn’t read the scriptures at all this whole week. God will understand” But at the end of the day, sin is sin in the eyes of the Lord. No matter how big or small the sin is. And we are only hurting ourselves when we rationalize our sins, and don’t repent.

    N E G A T I V I T Y
    When someone does something that hurts or upsets me, I try to see them how God sees them. I try to give them the benefit of the doubt. Most people don’t just go around actively trying to ruin other’s lives, right? There is a very small minority of people who do that. And the odds of us running into those people are very slim. So I try and see the motives, or background behind an action. Sometimes, people don’t even know that they’ve offended us, or done something wrong. Or sometimes we’ve grown up in a different environment, so something that seems odd and strange to us, may seem totally normal to someone else. So whenever I feel negative thoughts towards others, I try and step back and look at the big picture. Maybe this person was just simple having a bad day, and I caught them at the wrong time. Or maybe they did something truly on accident, and I should forgive them and give them the benefit of the doubt. And even if someone truly did something just to hurt me, or make me mad, then I will do my best to forgive them, and pray for them.
